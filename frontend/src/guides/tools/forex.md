## How It Works

Foreign exchange rates reflect the price of one currency in terms of another, determined by supply and demand in the global forex market — the largest financial market in the world, trading ~$7.5 trillion daily.

The **mid-market rate** (also called the interbank rate) is the midpoint between the buy (bid) and sell (ask) prices. This is what you see quoted on financial sites. When you convert currency at a bank or service, they add a **spread** on top — the difference between their rate and the mid-market rate is their profit.

Exchange rates fluctuate continuously based on factors including interest rate differentials between central banks, inflation expectations, trade balances, and geopolitical events.

**Cross rates** for less-traded pairs (e.g., GBP/JPY) are typically derived from their rates against USD rather than quoted directly.

## What Powers This

Live exchange rates are sourced from a currency exchange rate API updated in real time. Rates shown are mid-market rates. The conversion math is pure JavaScript arithmetic. Note: rates shown are for reference only and may differ from rates offered by financial institutions.
