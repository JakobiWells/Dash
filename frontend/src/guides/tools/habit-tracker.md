# Habit Tracker

Build consistency by tracking daily habits and watching your streaks grow.

## Adding a Habit

Click **+ Add habit** and type a name (e.g. *Exercise*, *Read*, *Meditate*). Press Enter to save.

## Logging a Habit

Each habit shows a row of day markers for the current week. Click the circle for today to mark it complete. The circle fills to indicate completion.

## Streaks

The tracker automatically counts your current streak (consecutive days completed). Missing a day resets the streak to 0.

## Editing & Deleting

Hover over a habit name to reveal edit and delete icons. Click the pencil to rename, or the trash to remove.

## Tips

- Keep habits specific and achievable — "Drink 8 glasses of water" works better than "Be healthy."
- Resize the card taller to track more habits without scrolling.
- Pair with the Pomodoro Timer and Todo List for a full daily productivity layout.

## How It Works

Habit tracking is grounded in **behavioral psychology**, specifically B.F. Skinner's operant conditioning and Charles Duhigg's habit loop model (cue → routine → reward). Tracking completion creates a visual **"streak"** — a continuous chain of successes — which leverages **loss aversion**: the psychological pain of breaking the chain becomes a motivation to maintain it.

**Streak calculation**: A streak is the count of consecutive days where a habit was marked complete, counting backwards from today. If today hasn't been logged yet, the streak extends through yesterday.

**Completion rate** = (completed days ÷ total days since start) × 100. Some trackers use a rolling window (last 30 or 90 days) instead of the all-time rate to better reflect recent behavior.

**Calendar heatmap visualization** (like GitHub's contribution graph) uses a grid where each cell represents a day. Color intensity maps to completion count. The grid is generated by computing the ISO week number and day-of-week for each date in the range.

## What Powers This

Habit data is stored in the browser's **localStorage** or **IndexedDB**, serialized as JSON. The calendar heatmap is rendered using custom SVG or Canvas. No external services are used — all your habit data stays in your browser.
